Setting up your user account

Contact information

This is your digital business card. It includes all the address-book fields that your partners will use to contact you, such as your phone numbers, email address and the physical location of your business.

It also includes a place to add a thumbnail-sized photograph of yourself. Photos enable partners who may have only met you once or twice to "put a face to the name." For best results, the photograph should be the same height and width. It should also be less than an inch tall.

Getting There To set up your contact information click on: Menu > My Account > Contact Info

Search Tags By default ReferralWire enables your partners to find you by typing any of the following into a list filter:

  • Your first name
  • You last name, or
  • You company name
You can also add custom search tags that enable partners to find you with other key words. For example, an accountant might add search tags such as "CPA," "Accountant," "Book Keeping," "Taxes," and "IRS" to his or her profile. The accountant's name will subsequently appear when any of these terms are typed into the Partner List filter.

To use this feature, simply type a list of terms into the Search Tags field separated by spaces. The Search Tags field will accept a maximum of 50 characters.

Qualifications

The qualifications page is a place to summarize your background, services and certifications. Your partners can refer to this information and show it to prospective customers. Before adding content to your qualifications page, you'll see placeholder images and sample text that indicate where content will appear. These will be replaced after you supply your own details. To add content, click the Edit button on the page header and the Qualifications Wizard will appear.

The qualifications view includes a place to add to a larger picture of yourself and an image of your company's logo. For best results, your picture should be approximately 1 inch wide and 1.5 inches tall. Your company's logo should be square and no more than 1 inch tall and wide.

Power Partners The qualifications page provides a place for you to declare up to three different "Power Partner" categories. These represent other types of businesses that provide complementary services to your clientele.

Getting There To set up your contact information click on: Menu > My Account > Qualifications

Printing your Qualifications To print your profile, click on the print icon . Your browser must accept pop up windows from ReferralWire for you to see the print-optimized window containing your profile. Note: At this time, the print feature is only supported on Chrome and Safari.

Testimonials

Testimonials showcase positive feedback from your customers, partners or other industry experts. You can add up to four separate testimonials. Each testimonial includes the following:
  • Quote Title This is a short excerpt from the quote that summarizes the key take-away. It will appear as a headline above the actual quote. Examples might include "Julie transformed our outdoor space..." or "Our revenues went up by 15%" or "Michael's input was invaluable" A good title is three to five words long.
  • Quote Text This is the actual verbatim quote from which the headline was extracted. A good quote is one to two sentences long.
  • Quote Source This names the source of the quote. At a minimum, the source should include the full name of the individual who volunteered the quote. It may also include the person's title or a description of the role he or she played when working with you -- e.g., "VP of Sales, Chevron Corporation" or "Father of the Bride" or "Seller - 312 Elm Street."

Getting There To set up your testimonials click on: Menu > My Account > Testimonials

Photos

You can upload pictures to your profile that showcase your services. Pictures may include any professional image that highlights your qualifications. Examples include:
  • Actual photographs of your work,
  • Logos from the business customers you serve,
  • Media coverage,
  • Charts and graphs that summarize important trends -- such as interest rates, or customer satisfaction survey results,
  • Pictures of you in front of an audience or with other, recognizable individuals in your field,
  • Informative medical or architectural diagrams,
  • Top 10 lists, and
  • Diplomas, awards and certification placards.

Getting There To set up your photo portfolio click on: Menu > My Account > Photos

Supported Files You can upload up to nine photos. The photos can use any standard image type -- i.e., .png, .jpg, or .gif. For best results, use pictures that are the same size and are large enough to expand to the full size of the screen.

Copyright Considerations It is your responsibility to adhere to all copyright restrictions in accordance with applicable laws. This obligation is included in ReferralWire's terms of service.

Preferences (Custom referral questions)

Partners who submit referrals to you fill out a form with standard information such as:
  • Name and contact information for the prospect
  • Comments
  • Whether they have a budget, decision making authority, etc.
Your partner will be prompted to provide answers to one to four custom questions determined by you. These custom questions enable you to collect more specific information to assess whether the prospect is a good potential customer for you and how to position your services to them.

Getting There To set up your preferences click on: Menu > My Account > Preferences

Password

ReferralWire enables you to change your password at any time. You may wish to do so periodically on your own or after receiving a system-generated password in the event you use the applications's forgot password feature.

To change your password, type in your current password followed by the new password. Confirm the new password by typing it in a second time. When finished, click Submit and you will be logged out of the application. Log back in using the new password.

Getting There To change your password click on: Menu > My Account > Password

Members, Contacts and My Partners

Members, Contacts, and My Partners

(All) Members are business people that use the ReferralWire application to co-sell their services with other members.

Contacts represent people that you know and might refer - either as a prospective customer or as a partner. You can add contacts by:
  • Creating them directly through the ReferralWire user interface;
  • Importing them from your smartphone address book; and
  • Importing them from Outlook, LinkedIn and other contact managers that support the .VCF file format.
My Partners are other ReferralWire members with whom you're connected. You can add new partners by:
  • Inviting other ReferralWire members to connect with you;
  • Inviting contacts you've imported to join as members; and
  • Accepting (power partner) introductions from your existing partners.

Your partners are automatically added to and included with your contacts.

Who can see my contacts?

Contacts added by you are not visible to anyone else unless you share them through a referral.

Adding more contacts

Contacts can be imported from multiple different sources (see the section in the document labeled Importing Contact). They can also be added, editted and deleted through ReferralWire's web application or through one of ReferralWire's smartphone applications.

Importing Contacts

Importing Contacts from LinkedIn

Contacts imported from LinkedIn or another address book represent your customers and other individuals who can be referred to your Partners. Partners by contrast, are fellow users of the ReferralWire application.

Platform Requirements Importing vcards is not supported on Internet Explorer 9.

Steps

  1. Log into LinkedIn.
  2. Go to http://www.linkedin.com/addressBookExport.
  3. Export the your contacts as "vCard (.vcf)."
  4. Within the ReferralWire app click on: Menu > Contacts
  5. Click on the "Import" button at the top of the Contact List page.
  6. When prompted, choose "vCards file" and select the file exported from LinkedIn.
  7. The import dialog window will present a list of Contacts from the LinkedIn file. You may choose which of these you want to import into ReferralWire.
  8. Click on the "Upload" button.

Importing Contacts from Outlook

Contacts imported from Outlook or another address book represent your customers and other individuals who can be referred to your Partners. Partners by contrast, are fellow users of the ReferralWire application.

Pre-requisites

You must be using Internet Explorer 9 or 10 (10 is preferred).

Steps

  1. Within Internet Explorer, open Internet Options (looks like a gear icon).
  2. Click on the "Security" tab.
  3. Click on the button labeled "Custom Level."
  4. Enable the option labeled "Initialize and script ActiveX controls not Marked as safe for scripting."
  5. Also enable the option labeled "Run ActiveX controls and plugin." Note: You browser may say this is an insecure option. Ignore it until you imported the outlook contacts.
  6. Within the ReferralWire app click on: Menu > Contacts
  7. Click on the "Import" button at the top of the Contact List page.
  8. The import dialog window will present a list of Contacts from Outlook. You may choose which of these you want to import into ReferralWire.
  9. Click on the "Upload" button.

Importing Contacts from a Smartphone

Contacts imported from your Smartphone's address book represent your customers and other individuals who can be referred to your Partners. Partners by contrast, are fellow users of the ReferralWire application.

Pre-requisites Download the ReferralWire Android application from Google Play -- or download the iPhone application from the Apple app store.

Steps

  1. Open up and log into the ReferralWire application on your phone.
  2. Open the expandable menu from the home page header and click on the Contacts button.
  3. After the Contact List appears, open the expandable menu again and click the Import button.
  4. When prompted, confirm that you wish to import the contacts from your phone's address book.

The smartphone contact import feature will import all of the contacts from your phone into your ReferralWire account. If there are contacts you wish to leave out, you can select and delete these individually after the import completes.

If you have a large number of contacts, the process may a couple minutes to complete.

Importing Contacts from a MacIntosh Address Book

Contacts imported from an existing address book represent your customers and other individuals who can be referred to your Partners. Partners by contrast, are fellow users of the ReferralWire application.

Steps

  1. In your Macintosh address book, find and select the contacts you wish to import into ReferralWire.
  2. Select multiple adjacent contacts by holding down the SHIFT key and using your mouse or trackpad to select a range of items. You can add other, individual contacts to your selection by holding the COMMAND key as you click on them.
  3. With the desired contacts selected, click on the File > Export > Export vCard... menu item. The resulting file will contain all of the selected contacts.
  4. Within the ReferralWire app click on: Menu > Contacts.
  5. Click on the "Import" button at the top of the Contact List page.
  6. When prompted, choose "vCards file" and select the file exported from your Mac Address book.
  7. The import dialog window will present a list of Contacts. You may choose which of these you want to import into ReferralWire.
  8. Click on the "Upload" button.

Giving and Tracking Referrals

Referrals: Who gets referred to whom?

ReferralWire enables users to make two different types of referrals:
  • Prospect Referrals provide the partner with the prospective customer's contact information and a brief explanation of why the prospect is in the market for the partner's services. The partner who receives the referral can then contact the prospect.
  • Power Partner Referrals introduce two businesses to each other under the assumption that the two parties would offer some mutual benefit to one another. Both parties are notified of the referral - and if they both accept, they will become connected as partners on ReferralWire.
Users can also make a Direct Invitation to an existing ReferralWire member or a contact. Invitations sent to contacts will generate an email with a link that allows the contact to register as a ReferralWire member.

Do the partners who receive the referral have to be ReferralWire members?

No - you can give referrals to non-members too - as long they have a valid email address, so they can be notified of the referral. For a given referral, a...
  • Partner  can be a ReferralWire member with whom you're connected, or one of your contacts with a valid email.
  • Prospective Customer  can be either one of your contacts (no email required) - or one of your partners - because partners can be customers too!

Creating a new Prospect Referral

Referrals can made directly from the home page by clicking on the button labeled Make a referral ! Referrals can also be initiated by selecting a contact or partner record and clicking the Refer button in the upper right corner. Each of these gestures opens the Referral Wizard. The Referral Wizard will guide you through selecting the parties whom you wish to connect - and providing them with the the reason for the referral.

  1. Step 1: Referral Type
    1. "What kind of referral is this?" Select the radio option: "I want to introduce a partner to a prospective customer."
    2. Click the Next button.
  2. Step 2: Who
    1. "Who's the customer (potential buyer)?" Select one of your contacts or one of your ReferralWire partners. This person's email and phone number will be shared with the partner.
    2. "Who's the partner (potential seller)?" Select one of your existing ReferralWire partners -- or one of your contacts (with a valid email address).
    3. "What's the partner's profession?" If you choose an existing ReferralWire partner - his or her professional category will automatically be used to answer this question. Otherwise, you'll need to select the partner's professional category. This answer is used to generate a list of potential reasons for the referral in the next step.
    4. Click the Next button.
  3. Step 3: Context
    1. "The primary reason [Prospect] needs help from [Partner] now is because [Prospect]": The answer helps the partner understand why the customer is in the market. Select one of the reasons - or choose "Other - see comments" and provide a note explaining why the referral is a timely one.
    2. "How soon is a response required?" Let your partner know whether an immediate response is required - or whether the timeframe is less urgent.
    3. Click the Next button.
  4. Step 4: Other
    1. [Custom Questions] If you've selected a ReferralWire member as the partner - and he or she has set up custom questions in their profile - you'll see a list of the questions here. Answer these to enable your partner to better qualify the prospect. If the partner you've selected isn't connected to you on ReferralWire - or doesn't have any custom questions set up in his or her profile - no custom questions will appear.
    2. "Is [Prospect] expecting a call from [Partner]" Check this box to confirm that you've told the prospect about the partner in advance.
    3. "Next Steps": The partner will be notified of the referral via email. If the partner is already a ReferralWire member, he or she will be able to log in directly and see the new referral on his or her home page. If the partner is not yet a ReferralWire member he or she will receive an email with an invitation to register. Once the partner has registered he or she will be able to accept the referral and see the details inside as an existing member.
    4. Click the Save button.

Creating a new Power Partner Referral

  1. Step 1: Referral Type
    1. "What kind of referral is this?" Select the radio option: "I want to introduce two partners to each other."
    2. Click the Next button
  2. Step 2: Who
    1. "Who's the first partner?" Select one of your existing ReferralWire partners -- or one of your contacts (with a valid email address).
    2. "Who's the first partner?" Select one of your existing ReferralWire partners -- or one of your contacts (with a valid email address).
    3. Click the Next button.
  3. Step 3: Context
    1. "Message from you to the partners" The message shows a simple explanation of why you believe that the two selected partners are a good match. The partners will see this message from you when they receive the referral.
    2. Click the Next button.
  4. Step 4: Other
    1. "Next Steps" Both partners will receive an email notification of the referral. If both accept, they will become connected to one another. If either or both are not already ReferralWire members, the email notification will include an invitation to register.
    2. Click the Save button.

The Lifecycle of a Prospect Referral

The status of every prospect referral changes based on updates made by the recipient. Possible status values are:
  • Not Accepted Yet This is initial value of a referral. This status remains until the receiving partner has explicitly accepted the referral.
  • Accepted The accepted status implies that the recipient has acknowledged the referral. Accepted referrals are eligible to be "Confirmed."
  • Confirmed Referral confirmation implies that the receiving partner has spoken with the prospect and that the prospect is interested. When referral status is updated to "Confirmed" the prospect becomes a contact of the receiving partner. This enables the receiving partner to forward the prospect on to others. It also causes a customer feedback email to be scheduled for delivery in 10 days. For more details see the section on Customer Feedback below. Confirmed referrals are eligible to be "Converted."
  • Converted When the referral status is set to "Converted" it implies that a sale has been made. The prospect is now a customer of referral recipient.
In addition to having a status updated by the partner who receives it, a referral record also shows whether the prospect approves of the partner to whom they've been referred. Feedback from the prospect is obtained through a very short email survey sent to the prospect 10 days after the referral has been confirmed. When the prospect responds to the survey the referral will be updated with a thumbs up or a thumbs down icon indicating that feedback was received.

The Lifecycle of a Power Partner Referral

The status of every power partner referral changes based on updates made by the recipients. Possible status values are as follows:
  • Not Accepted Yet This is initial value of a referral. For the recipient looking at the referral, this status indicates the referral is waiting for a response from him or her.
  • Waiting Indicates that one of the two partners have accepted.
  • Accepted The "Accepted" status implies that both recipients have accepted the referral and are now connected with one another.

Archiving and Un-archiving Referrals

You can archive a received referral at any time because there's no further action to take. Doing so will remove the referral from your inbox. Should you want to retrieve the referral later, it will be in a separate archive list which can be accessed by clicking on Menu > Referrrals and then clicking on the Archive tab on the lower right side of the page. Archived referrals can be restored by selecting them and clicking "Unarchive" from this page.

Tracking Updates Made by Your Partners

The referrals that you've given to your partners are visible in four separate places within the web application:
  • Home Page The panel titled: "Referrals from You: Recently Updated by your Partners" contains all the referrals that you've given that have been updated by your partners in the last two weeks. The records are sorted according to how recently they've been updated.
  • Referrals Given Page -- shows a complete list of all the referrals you've given. To go to the page:
    1. Click on Menu > Referrals
    2. Select the Given tab on the footer of the right side of the page.
  • Partner Referrals Page -- shows a list of all the referrals received from and given to a particular partner. To go to the page:
    1. Click on Menu > Partners.
    2. Select the partner record whose referral activity you'd like to see.
    3. Select the Referrals tab on the footer of the right side of the page.
  • Contact Referrals Page -- shows a list of all the partners to whom you've referred a particular contact. To go to the page:
    1. Click on Menu > Contacts.
    2. Select the contact record whose referral activity you'd like to see.
    3. Select the Referrals tab on the footer of the right side of the page.
The smartphone application includes a Referrals Given page similar to the one visible in the web application -- but filtered to only show those referrals that have been given in the last two weeks.

Other Questions

Supported Browsers and Operating Systems

Windows and MacIntosh: The ReferralWire application runs best on Google's Chrome browser, but is supported on all of the following browsers:
  • Chrome - version 25 and above
  • Safari - version 6 and above
  • Internet Explorer - version 9 and above
Apple iPad: The ReferralWire web application will run on Safari on an iPad and generally works well with a couple minor limitations:
  • The applications should be used with the landscape orientation (i.e., the device should be 'sideways'). The portrait orientation may cause some pages to be distorted and difficult to use.
  • File upload (e.g., for pictures) is not supported on the iPad.

Smartphones: ReferralWire has smartphone applications for both the Android and iOS (iPhone) operating systems. These are available for download and installation from Google Play and the Apple App Store respectively. Version support for these devices is as follows:

  • Android - The ReferralWire application is supported on most devices that are version 4 and above. Older Android phones may have some limitations with regard to scrolling or rendering large lists of data.
  • iPhone - The ReferralWire application is supported on the iPhone 4 and above (e.g., iPhone 4, 4S, or 5). The device should be running an iOS version that is 5 or above.

ReferralWire Usage Fees

ReferralWire is available to users at no charge. In subsequent releases, ReferralWire will add premium features that will require a paid subscription. At that time, users will have the option of signing up for the premium features or continuing to use just the free services.
